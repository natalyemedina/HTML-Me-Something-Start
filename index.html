Countries I Explored and Danced At

  • Malaga, Spain
  • Barcelona, Spain
  • Novi Sad, Serbia
  • Zagreb, Croatia
  • Split, Croatia
  • Budapest, Hungary
  • London, England

There is more to traveling than checking out instagrammable spots, partying, and tours. There's also Salsa dacing, for example. Social dancing and Salsa classes inspired me travel abroad and I made friends along the way.

Malaga, Spain

Malaga was my 1st favorite city in Europe. The acai bowls were to die for and the city is beautiful! My friend set up a hike at Caminito del Rey and it was breathtaking! I just couldn't wait to go Salsa dancing for the first time in Europe though! I fortunately met a girl at my hostel who loves dancing and we went to two socials together.

Barcelona, Spain

OK! Barcelona and Madrid are known for high level dancing and I had to check it out of course! I went to a Bachata class a friend recommended and was surprised to see the instructor was the famous instagram dancer Masahiro Nakagiri! I was very excited! I also participated in a couple of tours and found a piece of Santorini style homes in Barcelona!

Novi Sad, Serbia

I was eating at a vegan restaurant in Belgrade and asked my waiter for Salsa dancing recommendations. He asked some friends and got back to me about a Salsa Congress in Novi Sad, Serbia. I took the train the next day and met many people at the Salsa Congress. By the way, the best vegan food I tasted in the 11 countries I visited is in Belgrade! One of the people I met at the Salsa Congress told me about a Salsa festival in Hungary and another one told me about social dancing in Split, Croatia and so, I went.

Zagreb, Croatia

Zagreb! Oh my gosh Zagreb. The roads reminded me of France. IT IS BEAUTIFUL!!! I loved the public transportation system. I made it to two Salsa classes and a social. It was easy to connect with the locals and the travelers from the hostel. Oh, this sounds weird but there's a beautiful instagrammable cemeteray worth visiting.

Split, Croatia

We have made it to my favorite place on my trip! I fell in love with Split. I would move HERE! The people I met at the social were the friendliest! I loved that there was Salsa and Bachata classes Monday-Thursday. A girl I met in class invited me to join an all woman expat group and there, I made another friend. I explored with the locals and the people I met at the hostels when I wasn't at the socials and classes. The locals taught me a lot of Croatian words, they practiced the Spanish they learned from telenovelas with me, and they took me in as one of their own. I walked a bunch, explored castles, and lived a bit more of the hostel life. I loved the dancing and community so much I stayed a week and returned another week after visiting Dubrovnik and Montenegro.

Budapest, Hungary

Hungary wasn't on my list of countries to visit but I am glad I took that friend's recommendation. I went for a Salsa Congress and also got to explore the city. It is surprisingly beautiful. My favorite part, aisde from the Salsa Congress, was my tour of the city and reconnecting with a Salsa teacher and other dancers I met at the Novi Sad Salsa Congress. I also connected with two Salsa teachers from London at this Congress and they invited me to London for their classes.

London, England

We have made it to the last place I visited for dancing, London. London was scary. Everyone is in a rush! You have to be careful taking the tube! Finding a good hostel was also tough. Nonetheless, it's nice to say I was in London, even if it was just for a night. That was enough for me. Therefore, I don't have any pictures.
